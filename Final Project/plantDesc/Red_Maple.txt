Name: Red Maple
Scientific Name: Acer rubrum
Type: Tree
Size: 50 to 100 feet
Bloom Time: March to April
Fruit: Red and Brown Fruit
Bloom Color: Red

Synopsis:
The Red Maple is a staple tree and very well known. The leaves are ones that most would
associate with the Autumn season. Its bark was used centuries ago and even today to make
ink and dyes however most of the tree is considered poisonous. Most times, depending on
your area, moose and deer might be roaming to explore. Many birds and squirrels also call
a Red Maple's branches their home, certainly an ideal plant for any and all wildlife.
