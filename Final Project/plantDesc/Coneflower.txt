Name: Coneflower
Scientific Name: Dracopis amplexicaulis
Type: Herb
Size: 2 to 3 feet
Bloom Time: April to July
Fruit: None
Bloom Color: Yellow

Synopsis:
The Coneflower is an herb known for its unique flower, which has petals shying
away from the cone shaped center, causing the petals to droop. The Coneflower blooms
with an interesting combinatino of colors as the petals transition from a deep red at
the base to a birght sunshine yellow at the tips. It requires high maintenance and water
usage and were once used my the Cherokee to cure earaches. The Coneflower can live in
most kinds of soil and are considered a decorative flower, meant to brighten up and
add color to your garden.
