Name: Spice Bush
Scientific Name: Lindera Benzoin
Type: Shrub
Size: 6 to 12 feet
Bloom Time: April to October
Fruit: Red Berries
Bloom Color: Yellow

Synopsis:
The Spice Bush is a shrub that has multiple benefits. The flowers that bloom from this
plant are aromatic and conspicuous, making your yard smell wonderful. An interesting
use for this shrub is that it's leaves and twigs can be made into a tea while its berries
can be dried and powdered into a spice, thus it's name as the Spice Bush. It primarily
attracts butterflies to its flowers which can liven up any front yard or garden. With
only needing medium quantities of water, the Spice Bush can bring in some wonderful
color to any property.
