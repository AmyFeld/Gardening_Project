Name: Black-Eyed Susans
Scientific Name: Rudbeckia hirta
Type: Herb
Size: 1 to 2 feet
Bloom Time: June to October
Fruit: None
Bloom Color: Yellow

Synopsis:
The Black-eyed Susan is a herb that can make any garden into your own pocket prairie due to its daisy like shape. Its primarily role is to provide nectar for bees, butterflies, and other insects. The flower of the Black-Eyed Susan is daisy like in its petals however it has a dark, cone like center that protrudes outwards. Its known for warding off deer whlie attracting all kinds of bugs and birds. The Black-Eyed Susan only needs a moderate amount of water but will add a meadow like aesthetic to your garden.
