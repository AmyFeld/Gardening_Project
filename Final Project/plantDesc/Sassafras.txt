Name: Sassafras
Scientific Name: Sassafras albidum
Type: Tree
Size: 12 to 36 feet
Bloom Time: March to May
Fruit: Dark Berries
Bloom Color: Yellow

Synopsis:
The Sassafras, with its charming name, is an aromatic and useful tree. Its leaves and twigs are regularly sued for beverages such as root beers and teas and its dark berries, while edible, find their use in feeding local birds and the occasional black bear if in the right forest. The bark itself can be used to produce an orange dye and overall, the Sassafras is considered a very attractive tree from name to appearance.
