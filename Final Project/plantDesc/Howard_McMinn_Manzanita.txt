Name: Howard McMinn Manzanita
Scientific Name: Arctostaphylos densiflora
Type: Tree
Size: 5 to 6 feet
Bloom Time: March to April
Fruit: Edible Fruit
Bloom Color: Pink

Synopsis:
The Howard McMinn Manzanita is a tree with distinct reddish stems growing. It's an easy addition to any
garden, being a shorter tree, and is a showy plant with its light pink blooms. All sorts of wildlife
adore the Howard McMinn Manzanita, such as butterflies, hummingbirds and moths. If you'd like, you can
harvest the fruit that this stree produces and eat it! This tree makes an excellent hedge decoration and
will add a beautiful touch to any garden.
